K2-9b

K2-9b is an exoplanet discovered during the Kepler telescope's K2 mission. It is orbiting an M2.5-type red dwarf, about 110 parsecs (approximately 360 light years) away from Earth. This planet has a radius approximately 2.25 times that of Earth and an orbital period of 18.45 days. Its surface temperature is less than 500 K, which indicates that it is not an extremely hot planet. However, the ultraviolet radiation it receives from its host star is strong enough to affect its atmosphere, reducing the chances of it being habitable. Regarding its location in the Milky Way, K2-9b is located in the Orion Arm or in the vicinity of it, since this is the arm in which a large part of the stars observed during the K2 mission are located.
