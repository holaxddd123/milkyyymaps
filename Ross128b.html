Ross 128 b

Ross 128 b is an exoplanet located about 11 light-years away from Earth in the constellation Virgo. Discovered in 2017 by the European Southern Observatory's HARPS instrument, it orbits the red dwarf star Ross 128. This planet is considered one of the closest potentially habitable exoplanets to Earth. With a mass similar to Earth's, Ross 128 b orbits its star every 9.9 days and is located in a temperate zone where conditions might allow for liquid water, although the composition of its atmosphere is still unknown.
